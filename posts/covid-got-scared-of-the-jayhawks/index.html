On March 17, 2020, the NCAA canceled the men’s basketball championships due to the ongoing rise of the coronavirus. At the time, the three-time champions were hoping to make it a fourth in Lawrence.

As the 2020 college basketball season approached, analysts were setting their preseason odds for teams across the nation. Among the favorites were Michigan State, Kentucky, Duke, and Kansas. The Jayhawks entered the season with high expectations and one of the most complete rosters they’ve had since the glory days with Wilt Chamberlain.

Although Bill Self’s squad was not the clear favorite, they were hunting for greatness, defying the odds of anyone who stepped in their path. When the season kicked off on Tuesday, November 5, 2019, Kansas entered with +950 odds to win it all.

As the season progressed, one question kept coming up: which conference was the best? With dominant players on talented rosters, it was clear the answer would come from the Power Five.

On the East Coast, many would point to the ACC, home to some of the best coaches to ever walk the sidelines. Others argued the Big Ten was on the rise, bringing a mix of talent and strategy. But the most accurate way to settle the debate? The numbers. Conference ranking can be measured by Strength of Schedule (SOS) and Simple Rating System (SRS) grades, revealing which leagues truly tested their teams and who dominated their competition throughout the season.

With a 28-3 record the Jayhawks didn’t just play the best teams, they beat them convincingly. With an SRS nearing 25 and their SOS just shy of 12, Kansas proved they belonged as a one seed heading into the madness.

Even though records, stats and grading systems show us a lot, do they share the full story with us? Polls play a huge role in shaping where teams land in March. Coaches and the Associated Press had their eyes on Kansas from the start, but where did they land when covid ruined it all?

The 2020 Men’s National Champion… The Kansas Jayhawks. Well that is what would have been said if Covid-19 didn’t knock out all of Lawrence’s competition. With all the numbers, polls, stats and gradings system there isn’t a whole lot saying Bill Self wouldn’t have added another ring to his collection.

With the lights set to shine on at Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta, Georgia on April 6, 2020 would the Jayhawks bring home another title, or would this have been one of the greatest “what if” teams in history? The truth is, we’ll never know, but let’s speculate and hang up a banner to remember this historic season and honor the team that brought us back to the Holy Land.
